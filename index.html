Vietnam has full, undeniable sovereignty over Hoang Sa, Truong Sa archipelagos

Evidence contained in historical documents and continuous management:

  • 17th century: The two archipelagos were called “Bai Cat Vang” (Golden Sandbank) of Binh Son district, Quang Ngai province".
  • Many historical documents of Vietnam in the 17th century proved that the Vietnamese exploited two archipelagos:
    • The Miscellaneous Chronicles of Pacification in the Border Area (1776).
    • The Prequel Records of Dai Nam (1844-1848).
    • History of the Unified Dai Nam (– 1865-1875).
    • Imperial records of the Nguyen Dynasty (1802-1945),
  • 1816: King Gia Long sent troops to hoist a Vietnamese flag in Hoang Sa archipelago.
  • 1884: The French administration in Indo-China continued to observe and strengthen Vietnam’s sovereignty over Hoang Sa and Truong Sa archipelagos.
